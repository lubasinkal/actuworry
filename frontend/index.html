Understanding Present Value

Present value (PV) discounts future money back to today. The formula is:

PV = FV / (1 + i)^n

Where FV is future value, i is interest rate, n is years. This is fundamental to all actuarial calculations.

What is an Annuity?

An annuity is a series of equal payments made at regular intervals.

  • • Whole life annuity - payments for life
  • • Temporary annuity - payments for fixed term
  • • Deferred annuity - payments start in future
  • • Annuity-due - payments at beginning of period

Understanding Monte Carlo in Actuarial Science

Monte Carlo simulation models uncertainty by generating thousands of random scenarios. For insurance:

  • • Interest rate risk modeling
  • • Asset-liability management
  • • Solvency capital requirements

VaR (Value at Risk) - Maximum expected loss at a confidence level.

CTE (Conditional Tail Expectation) - Average loss in worst scenarios.

Understanding Risk Measures

VaR 95%

Loss that will only be exceeded 5% of the time. Regulatory standard for capital.

VaR 99%

Stricter standard - only 1% chance of exceedance. Used for solvency.

CTE

Expected loss given that we exceed VaR. More sensitive to tail risk.

Nominal Rate (i^(m))

Stated rate with m compounding periods per year. i^(m)/m is the rate per period.

Effective Rate (i)

Actual yearly return when interest is compounded. (1 + i^(m)/m)^m - 1

Force of Interest (δ)

Continuous compounding limit. δ = ln(1 + i)
